Porsche Cayenne Turbo Coupé

Here's all you really need to know about the 2020 Porsche Cayenne Turbo Coupe: With its 541-hp twin-turbo 4.0-liter V-8, eight-speed automatic transmission, and all-wheel drive, the new fastback version of Porsche's largest SUV is mechanically identical to the conventional Cayenne Turbo squareback, and the two vehicles feel exactly the same from behind the wheel. Same brutal acceleration, same impressive driving dynamics, same big fat grin on your face when you mat their accelerators. With its 0.7-inch lower roofline and 0.7-inch wider rear fenders, the coupe looks a touch better than the SUV, which is the point. It also costs $2300 more at $131,450 to start, albeit $34,300 less than the top-dog 670-hp Turbo S E-Hybrid model that we've already driven. Opting for the Turbo Coupe over the regular Cayenne Turbo nets you four seats as standard (a three-across rear bench is optional), and you lose six cubic feet of cargo space, although the rear seat is 1.2 inches lower to compensate for the lower roofline. None of that will matter much once you see it in the metal, though. This fastback SUV makes its key rivals, the BMW X6 M and Mercedes-AMG GLE63 S Coupe, look like they were designed in the dark.
